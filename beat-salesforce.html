Beating Salesforce in a Competitive Deal

Salesforce wins on brand and ecosystem. You win on speed, adoption, and total cost. Here is the exact playbook to flip the narrative before the deal closes.

Where you win against Salesforce
Faster time-to-value Lower TCO Higher rep adoption Less admin overhead No SI dependency
Top objections & how to handle them
“We already use Salesforce.”
Ask about their actual daily active usage rate. Most orgs are at 40–60%. That gap is your opening — the tool they pay for isn't the tool their reps use.
“Salesforce is the industry standard.”
Industry standard means common, not right. Ask: what business outcome haven't you achieved since going live? Implementation drag and shelfware are both real.
“Our IT team requires Salesforce.”
IT chooses infra. Revenue leaders choose sales tools. Involve VP Sales and Sales Ops directly. Frame it as a pilot — 10 reps, 90 days, measurable pipeline outcome.
Battlecard: key moves in the deal
1Ask: "What is your current rep adoption rate in Salesforce?" Most orgs answer 40–60%. That gap is your value story.
2Implementation anchor: Salesforce averages 6–9 months to full deployment. What is your time-to-value comparison?
3True TCO: Salesforce licenses + admin salary ($80–120K) + SI/consulting fees. Build the 18-month model live.
4Pull 2 customer wins where you displaced Salesforce in the same industry — social proof closes faster than features.
