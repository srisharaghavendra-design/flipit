Beating Gong in a Competitive Deal

Gong wins on conversation intelligence brand and North America dominance. Your openings are APAC compliance, integrated forecasting, and full platform vs point solution.

Top objections & how to handle them
“Gong gives us call recording and coaching.”
Ask: are they using Gong for forecasting or just recording? Most orgs use less than half of what they pay for. The recording use case is commoditised — the real ROI question is pipeline accuracy.
“Gong is the standard for revenue intelligence.”
In North America, yes. In APAC and EU, data residency laws and call recording consent requirements make Gong legally complex. Ask where their customer-facing teams are based.
“We have built coaching workflows in Gong.”
Acknowledge the investment. Then ask: what is your current forecast accuracy, and how long did it take to get there? That is where the ROI gap lives and where you win the conversation.
Battlecard: key moves in the deal
1APAC and EU angle: Gong has data residency issues in India, Japan, Singapore, and EU-regulated industries. Lead with compliance risk first.
2Ask: "What is your forecast accuracy today?" Then show how an integrated platform closes the gap without separate tooling cost.
3Coaching ROI is hard to measure. Pipeline accuracy ROI is measurable in one quarter. Shift the value conversation.
4Stack consolidation: replacing Gong plus a forecasting tool plus CRM analytics with one platform is a strong TCO story.
